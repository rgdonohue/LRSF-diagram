Retail, Restaurant, and Packaged Goods

Equitable Response and Recovery

Impacts and Response in LRFS:

  • Small, independent operators, local restaurants, food entrepreneurs, and independent grocers are uniquely impacted by disruptions like COVID-19 and climate crises because of limited access to cash reserves to weather the storm.
  • Owner operators and employees of these businesses are disproportionately impacted by the front-line risk of continuing essential food access services.
  • In the case of incubator kitchens, there are significant populations of new-immigrant and historically marginalized entrepreneurs.

Resilience in Action:

  • The Nourish Our Neighbors program run by Hope & Main Food Incubator in Rhode Island was launched in response to increased levels of food insecurity brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic. Through a buy one/give one model, the program provided meals to food insecure families and vulnerable individuals.

Securing Supply Chains

Impacts and Response in LRFS:

  • With critical access to power (Esp. for cold storage), gas, and transportation of goods, locally embedded operations can serve as points of emergency service provision and maintain continuity of markets. This, in turn, has significant consequences for the safety and quality of food and community food access.
  • Without access to affordable and scale-appropriate online sales platforms, independent food retailers and value-added producers struggle to pivot to online and alternative sales platforms.

Resilience in Action:

  • Community Food Coops are Thriving During the Pandemic, National Coop Grocers: Independent grocers and coops that had established relationships with producers and suppliers were better able to keep shelves full in times of supply chain shortage.
  • New Orleans' Get Lit Stay Lit initiative is one example of how using a local and regional food system-level response, restaurants coordinated with response efforts in their communities to use existing cold storage infrastructure during climate-related food systems disruptions.

Maintaining Markets

Impacts and Response in LRFS:

  • When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, restaurants faced dire financial and operational circumstances as businesses closed because of lockdowns.
  • Restaurant closures significantly disrupted the relationships between local producers and restaurants. Overnight, farmers lost valuable markets as restaurants struggled to maintain operations.
  • Value-added food entrepreneurs who are commonly reliant on direct-to-consumer markets or sales to independent grocers and restaurants are forced to pivot to entirely new sales models in order to reach consumers amid disruption.

Resilience in Action:

  • Restaurant Reboot Relief Program Innovation Brief: The Lee Initiative provided stipends to local farms in 20 regions across the U.S. to ensure continued food production for restaurants and to maintain producer-restaurant relationships. This allowed restaurants to lower ingredient costs while still acquiring local products, providing high-quality food to patrons, retaining restaurant workers, and maintaining markets for and relationships with farmers.
